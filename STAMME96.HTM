Brenner Anton was born in 1816 in Uttingsdorf as son of
  • Brenner Anton, born in 1788, an_abatoir worker from Pettendorf
  • and Bieberle Maria Viktoria, born in 1785 of Uttingsdorf
Uttingsdorf is located 70mi north of Brno in the Check Republic.

He married at the age of 25 years in the year 1841 Jenesch Helene Maria -, 3 years younger -, born in 1819 from Uttingsdorf. He was a_pub landlord and the couple lived in Groissenbrunn. About 11 years later had their son Karl, born in 1852.

He died at the age of 46 years in the year 1862 in Groissenbrunn 35 years earlier than his wife. Mourning were amongst others his wife Helene.

He lived in the epoch of Classicism and as a young fellow under the reign of the Emperor Ferdinand I of Habsburg-Lothringen. Some of his contemporaries of a similar age are Abraham Lincoln, Charles Darwin and Frédéric Chopin. During his life there were many wars, amongst them the Sardinian-Austrian war, the Hungarian Revolution and the Sardinian war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during his time are the mouth organ, the raw material for the paper production, the light bulb and the telephone.

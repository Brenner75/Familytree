Brenner Juliane was born in the zodiac sign of Leo on a Friday, the 22.08.1884 in Gaisruck as daughter of
  • Fehringer Franz, born in 1844, a_sheperd from Gettsdorf
  • and Haslinger Theresia, born in 1847 of Niederrussbach
Gaisruck is a little village in the northern part of Austria.

She had 7 bretheren: Franz, Karl, Barbara, born in 1870, Therese, Marie, Katharina and Anna. She married at the age of 25 years in the year 1909 in Hausleiten Brenner Carl Richard -, 2 years and 2 months older -, a_milk trader, born in 1882 from Stockerau. She was a_grocery shop owner and the couple lived in Wien_Capistrangasse. They had 2 children: Karl Franz, born in 1910 and Franz, born in 1913. In the year 1920 she mourned the death of her husband Carl.

Not a lot is known about her early life, but after the untimely death of her husband (she was a widow when she was 36 years old), she ran a milk shop in the 6th district of Vienna. She was a very proud woman and the saying goes, that she only greeted her customers when they entered her shop but never, when she walked the streets and met one of them. She was a stout woman with interesting green eyes and loved to wear a fox skin, which was made in such a way, that the foxe's jaw could bite his own tail in order to stay properly in place around the proud woman's neck. She had no gloves, but a 'Muff', which was basically a big fur tube with openings each end for the hands. She had quite a noticable moustache, which she used to burn off with a candle before she met up with a very elusive character, called Mr. Brillinger, who was obviousely her partner but somehow never wanted to admit to it. During her later years she suffered from very heavy diabetes, which had caused an open wound on her leg, which refused to heal and finally led to her death.

She died at the age of 77 years on a Wednesday, the 04.10.1961 in Wien 41 years later than her husband of diabetes. Mourning were amongst others her son Karl, her granddaughter Hertha Zobl, her brother in law Franz, her sister in law Karoline, her daughter in law Alice, her nephews Franz Rauscher and Karl.

She lived in the epoch of Historicism and as a young girl under the reign of the Emperor Franz-Joseph I of Habsburg-Lothringen. Some of her contemporaries of a similar age are Mata Hari and Agatha Christie. During her life there were many wars, amongst them the Great war, the Second World war and the Korean war.

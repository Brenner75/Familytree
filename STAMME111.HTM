Binder Anna was born in the zodiac sign of Pisces on a Saturday, the 22.02.1845 in Budapest as daughter of
  • Gindra Anton, born in 1810 from Ungarn
  • and Bloszovitz Magdalena, born in 1820 of Budapest
Budapest is the Hungarian capital.

She had 2 bretheren: Hermine, born in 1849 and Fanny. She married at the age of 25 years on a Saturday, the 16.10.1870 in Baden_St._Stefan Binder Josef Johann -, 4 years older -, a_waiter, born in 1840 from Wien F nfhaus. She was a_pub landlady and the couple lived in Baden. They had 6 children: Emilia, born in 1869, Karl, born in 1871, Josef, born in 1873, Johanna, born in 1875, Hans, born in 1877 and Michael, born in 1880. In the year 1880 she mourned the death of her husband Josef. In the year 1913 she stood by the grave of her child Johanna.

She died at the age of 71 years on a Friday, the 30.06.1916 in Baden about 36 years later than her husband. Mourning were amongst others her son Hans, her daughter Emilia Mock, her granddaughter Alice Körber, her son in law Albrecht Mock.

She lived in the epoch of Historicism and as a young girl under the reign of the Emperor Franz-Joseph I of Habsburg-Lothringen. During her life there were many wars, amongst them the Sardinian-Austrian war, the Hungarian Revolution and the Sardinian war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during her time are the light bulb, X-rays, the Aspirin pills, and the spark plug.

Brenner Karl was born in the zodiac sign of Aquarius on a Sunday, the 06.02.1910 in Gaisruck as son of
  • Brenner Carl Richard, born in 1882, a_milk trader from Stockerau
  • and Fehringer Juliane, born in 1884, a_grocery shop owner of Gaisruck
Gaisruck is a little village in the northern part of Austria.

He had a brother Franz, 2 years and 10 months younger, born in 1913. The father died when little Karl was 10 years old. He married at the age of 26 years in the year 1936 in Aderklaa KOERBER Alice Anna Anna -, 6 years younger -, a_housewife, born in 1916 from Aderklaa. He was a_civil servant and the couple lived in Wien. They had 2 children: Karl Georg Emil, born in 1949 and Hertha, born in 1938.

He attended first school in Pettendorf and went to Grammar school in Stockerau until 1929, after which he began to study Geography and History at the University of Vienna. But since his father had died very early there was not enough money in the family to pay for his studies and he had to give it up and take a job in a government office, where he earned 120 Schillings a month (about 10 Pounds in today's money) and started to climb the carreer ladder until the war started and he was conscripted in 1939, shortly after he had married and had fathered his daughter. During the war he became financial resource manager for the supply chain to the various fronts in Poland, the Netherlands, Belgium, France, Romania, Bulgaria, Serbia and Greece. In 1941 he was caught and sent to war prison in Russia in a cattle wagon. For 3 days the train was parked in Deutsch-Wagram, only a short distance from where his family lived at this time. He smuggled a note out of his waggon saying: "I am here, but where is my family?" He was imprisoned in the Ukraine but a cow hit him on the shin so badly that he was released and returned home in 1943. He loved cows ever since. In 1949 he accidentally fathered his second child and was not pleased about it. In the Fifties he moved to Vienna with his family and became a fairly high goverment officer managing some 80 people in the controlling department. After his retirement he moved back to Deutsch-Wagram where he tended the garden enthusiastically until his death. All his life he had been a brilliant piano player.

He died at the age of 83 years on a Saturday, the 06.02.1993 in Wien 19 years earlier than his wife of a stroke. Mourning were amongst others his wife Alice, his son Karl, his daughter Hertha Zobl, his grandchildren Michael, Thomas Zobl and Marcus Zobl, his granddaughter Martina Askell, his sister in law Hertha Seil, his son in law Engelbert Zobl, his daughter in law Franziska, his nephew Kurt, his cousin Franz Rauscher as well as further members of the family.

He lived in the epoch of modern times and as a young fellow under the reign of the Emperor Karl I of Habsburg-Lothringen. Some of his contemporaries of a similar age are Louis Armstrong, Walt Disney and Salvadoire Dali. During his life there were many wars, amongst them the Great war, the Second World war and the Korean war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during his time are the 'Goldbärchen' sweet, the compact camera, the tape recorder and the tea bag.

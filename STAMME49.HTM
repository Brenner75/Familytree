Brenner Hermine was born in the zodiac sign of Pisces on a Saturday, the 20.03.1852 in Holitz/M hren as daughter of
  • Marek Karl, born in 1812 from Landskron
  • and Fiedler Barbara, born in 1825 of Olmütz
Holitz (Holice) lies in the Check Republic 60mi south of Brno.

She had a sister Karolina, 6 years younger, born in 1858. She married at the age of 27 years in the year 1879 in Wien Landstra e Brenner Karl - 5 months younger -, a_cobbler, born in 1852 from Leopoldsdorf. She was a_cook and the couple lived in Stockerau. They had 8 children: Carl Richard, born in 1882, Helene, born in 1875, Franz, born in 1880, Anton, born in 1875, Julie, born in 1875, Anna, born in 1875, Hermine, born in 1875 and Karoline, born in 1889. In the year 1933 she mourned the death of her husband Karl. In the year 1920 she stood by the grave of her child Carl.

She died at the age of 86 years on a Friday, the 23.09.1938 in Stockerau 5 years later than her husband. Mourning were amongst others her son Franz, her daughter Karoline, her grandchildren Karl and Franz, her daughter in law Juliane.

She lived in the epoch of Historicism and as a young girl under the reign of the Emperor Franz-Joseph I of Habsburg-Lothringen. During her life there were many wars, amongst them the Sardinian war, the Italien Independence war and teh German-Danish war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during her time are the light bulb, Blue Jeans, the bakteriology and the tram.

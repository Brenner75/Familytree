Kuhn Elisabeth was born in 1901 in Parbasdorf as daughter of
  • Körber Emil, born in 1864, a_school master from Jägerndorf
  • and Schmarda Wilhelmine, born in 1863, a_craft teacher of Spannberg
Parbasdorf is a smallholding in the Marchfeld east of Vienna.

She had 3 bretheren: Emil Karl, born in 1888, Ernst, born in 1890 and Emma, born in 1893. She married propably in the year 1925 Kuhn Franz -, 1 years younger -, a_comb manufacturer, born in 1899 from Oberösterreich. She was a_craft teacher and the couple lived in Kleinreifling.

She died at the age of 90 years on a Wednesday, the 11.09.1991 in Altersheim of old age. Mourning were amongst others her nieces Alice Brenner and Hertha Seil as well as further members of the family.

She lived in the epoch of Historicism and as a young girl under the reign of the Emperor Franz-Joseph I of Habsburg-Lothringen. Some of her contemporaries of a similar age are Marlene Dietrich, Astrid Lindgren and Mutter Theresa. During her life there were many wars, amongst them the Great war, the Second World war and the Korean war. Amongst important discoveries and inventions during her time are the spark plug, the 'Goldbärchen' sweet, the compact camera and the tape recorder.
